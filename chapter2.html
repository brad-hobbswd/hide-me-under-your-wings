Chapter 2: The Cave Season Nobody Knew About

After that night in her car, Ashley entered what she later called her cave season. Unlike David's cave, Ashley's cave existed in quiet places hidden from everyone else. Spaces where nobody saw the pain. Spaces where life grew silent. Spaces where her soul finally breathed.

She slowly withdrew. Calls went unanswered. Plans were canceled. Social events disappeared from her schedule. Others thought she was distant. Others assumed she was pulling away. Nobody realized she was fighting invisible battles.

Exhaustion had settled deeply. She felt emotionally drained and desperately needed refuge.

Her cave became long drives with no destination. Late nights sitting on her bedroom floor. Tears falling beside an open Bible. Soft whispers to Jesus.

"Keep me as the apple of Your eye; hide me under the shadow of Your wings." Psalm 17:8

Those words became more than scripture. Those words became oxygen. Ashley discovered that hiding in God's presence was not running from life. Refuge gave her strength until her heart could breathe again.

Her cave forced honesty. Anger surfaced. Regret surfaced. Grief surfaced. Shame surfaced. Every emotion she buried suddenly demanded attention.

"The Lord is near to the brokenhearted and saves those who are crushed in spirit." Psalm 34:18

Ashley repeated those words constantly. Brokenness no longer felt like disqualification. Brokenness became an invitation into deeper closeness with Jesus.

Healing refused to rush. Healing refused shortcuts. Healing slowly rebuilt every damaged place.

"My grace is sufficient for you, for My power is perfected in weakness." 2 Corinthians 12:9

Weakness stopped feeling like failure. Weakness became the place where God's strength met her deepest need.

To the reader walking through a cave season:

Hidden seasons are not wasted seasons. Caves often become classrooms. Jesus still covers every fear, every lonely night, and every hidden tear. The place that feels hidden today may become the place where strength rises tomorrow.
