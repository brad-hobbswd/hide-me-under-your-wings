Chapter 3: When Words Become Weapons

Ashley thought her hardest battles had already happened. She had begun finding refuge beneath Jesus' wings. She had discovered quiet healing and hidden strength. Then another battle arrived. This battle came through words.

Words spoken by someone she loved. Words from someone trusted. Words intended to correct yet filled with judgment. Words intended to help yet delivered through misunderstanding.

Every sentence felt sharp. Every phrase seemed to strike places inside her heart that had barely begun healing. Ashley replayed the conversations repeatedly. She questioned herself. She questioned every decision. She wondered whether she had failed somehow.

"For it was not an enemy who reproached me... But it was you, a man my equal, my companion and my familiar friend." Psalm 55:12-14

Betrayal hurt differently. Pain from strangers felt one way. Pain from trusted people felt entirely different. Ashley felt herself wanting to retreat. Wanting to disappear. Wanting silence.

Yet instead of withdrawing from God, she withdrew into Him. She cried. She prayed. She poured out every emotion.

"Lord Jesus, hide me beneath Your wings because this pain feels too heavy."

A realization slowly surfaced. Not every battle requires confrontation. Sometimes protection becomes victory. Sometimes peace becomes victory. Sometimes surrender becomes victory.

"The righteous cry out, and the Lord hears, and delivers them out of all their troubles." Psalm 34:17

Ashley discovered that feelings and truth were not always the same thing. Feelings whispered rejection. Truth declared love. Feelings whispered failure. Truth declared purpose. Feelings whispered worthlessness. Truth declared identity.

She began speaking scripture aloud daily. She prayed for the people who wounded her. Bitterness slowly lost strength. Healing slowly gained ground.

To the reader carrying pain from words:

Words may wound deeply. Misunderstanding may cut deeply. Betrayal may leave lasting scars. Yet Jesus remains greater than every accusation and every painful voice. His wings remain large enough to cover every place where hurt attempted to settle.

Your identity remains safe in Him.
